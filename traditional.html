Portuguese Caldo Verde

Caldo Verde has a long history in Portuguese cuisine. It originated in the northern region of Portugal, particularly in the Minho province. The soup is believed to have been created by shepherds who used simple ingredients readily available to them, such as potatoes, onions, and kale. Over time, Caldo Verde became a popular dish throughout the country and is now considered one of Portugal's national dishes. It's loved for its comforting flavor and is often enjoyed during festive occasions or as a hearty meal.

Caldo Verde is a super tasty Portuguese soup made with potatoes, onions, kale, and sometimes chorizo. It's warm, flavorful, and perfect for chilly days

Idli

From the 7th to the 12th century, many Hindu kings ruled Indonesia and when they used to come to India to meet their relatives during holidays or find brides for themselves, they also used to bring their royal chefs with them. This is how the Indonesian recipe of Kedlis came to India and got adapted as Idlis.

Idli is a soft, pillowy steamed savory cake made from fermented rice and lentil batter Idlis are light on the stomach and super healthy at the same time. Steamed Idlis are best paired with Sambhar and this combination is perfect for all meals- breakfast, lunch and even dinner.

Fajitas

Fajitas have grown from obscure, South Texas roots to become a beloved mainstay of Mexican cooking. The history of fajitas, while somewhat blurred, belongs to the Rio Grande Valley of South Texas and northern Mexico, where skirt steak was often used as payment to vaqueros (Spanish for “cowboys”). The vaqueros soon became adept at marinating the steak and devouring its various, tasty forms. The mainstream popularity of fajitas truly began in the 70s as restaurants and concession stands in South Texas awakened the cowboy tradition by grilling skirt steak on sizzling platters and serving them with flour tortillas. Soon, thanks to some very satisfied and loyal customers, their popularity exploded. Soon enough, they became a staple at Mexican restaurants across the state, with many variations on this simple dish.

A fajita in Tex-Mex cuisine, is any stripped grilled meat, optionally served with stripped peppers and onions usually served on a flour or corn tortilla. The term originally referred to skirt steak, the cut of beef first used in the dish. Popular alternatives to skirt steak include chicken and other cuts of beef, as well as vegetables instead of meat. Popular condiments include shredded lettuce, sour cream, guacamole, salsa, pico de gallo, shredded cheese, refried beans, and diced tomatoes. "Tacos de arrachera" is applied to the northern Mexican variant of the dish.

red-bean-buns

Red bean buns, also known as "anpan," have a rich history in Japanese cuisine. They were introduced to Japan by Portuguese traders in the 16th century, along with other Western ingredients and cooking techniques. Over time, red bean buns became a beloved and iconic sweet treat in Japan. They are now widely enjoyed and can be found in bakeries and cafes across the country

Red bean buns, also known as "anpan," are a popular Japanese pastry filled with sweet red bean paste. They have a soft and fluffy texture, and the red bean filling provides a slightly sweet and nutty flavor. These buns are often enjoyed as a snack or dessert in Japan and are loved for their delicious taste and comforting appeal.

Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert with a rich history. It is believed to have originated in the region of Veneto in Italy during the 1960s. The exact origin and creator of tiramisu are still debated, but it is widely enjoyed and loved around the world today. Tiramisu is made with layers of ladyfingers soaked in coffee, creamy mascarpone cheese, and dusted with cocoa powder. It's a decadent and delicious treat!

Tiramisou is a heavenly Italian dessert! It consists of layers of ladyfingers soaked in coffee, creamy mascarpone cheese, and a dusting of cocoa powder on top. The combination of flavors and textures creates a delightful treat that is both rich and light. It's the perfect balance of sweetness and coffee goodness!

Churros

While they are often associated with Spain, churros actually have roots in Portugal as well. They were introduced to Spain by Portuguese sailors and became popular in both countries. Churros have since spread to many other parts of the world and are enjoyed as a sweet treat.

Churros are a delicious Spanish treat made from fried dough that's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. They're often dusted with sugar and served with a warm chocolate dipping sauce. They're absolutely yummy!
